CS2100 Summary

Summary of everyting taught in CS2100 condensed into 7 pages. This resource was made by me, so please do not reproduce.
I took CS2100 in AY2020/2021 Sem 1. The first half of the module (before recess week) was manageable, but I struggled to grasp some of the concepts taught in the second half of the module. For the online midterm and final, we were allowed to bring in 10 sheets of cheatsheet. The exams consisted of MCQ, MRQ and short answer questions, and they mostly tested applications of concepts learnt.
Overall, it was a good introduction to what goes on inside our computer, though the module is challenging at times.

MA1101R Summary

Summary of the main concepts taught in MA1101R condensed into 3 pages. This resource was made by me, so please do not reproduce.
I took MA1101R in AY2020/2021 Sem 1. It is one of the most challenging modules I have taken so far. Many theorems were taught and I often felt overwhelmed and lost while taken this module as I was unsure which theorem to apply to solve problems. The assingments were manageable. However, the final was challenging and I felt that insufficient time was given. The online final was open book, but you will probably not have enough time to search your notes if you do not already know what you are searching for. The exams consisted of about 5 questions that had multiple parts. There was no midterm the semester I took it.
Overall, it is probably a useful module for certain higher level modules, but I felt like too much content was being taught within a short span of time. If the concepts taught in MA1101R are needed for modules I take in future, I will probably have to spend so time re-looking them.

EC1301 (Micro) Summary

Summary of microeconomics key points taught in EC1301 condensed into 2 pages. This summary is not comprehensive and highlights the main concepts taught. This resource was made by me, so please do not reproduce.
I took EC1301 in AY2020/2021 Sem 1. The first half of the module focused on microeconomics while the second half focused on macroeconomics. The microeconomics portion of this module mostly taught concepts that I had already learnt while taking H2 Economics in JC. It provides a foundation for higher level modules such as EC2101. The online midterm and final were closed book and consisted of only MCQ questions. Most of the answers to the questions can be taken directly from the lectures or found through simple calculations. If you understand the concepts and can apply them well, both the midterm and final should be very manageable. The bell curve for this module is hence really steep.
Overall, EC1301 teaches foundational economic concepts that will be very useful for higher level modules. The content is pretty similar to H2 Econs for the microeconomics portion, but the macroeconomics portion does introduce quite a few new ideas.

CS2105 Summary

Summary of everyting taught in CS2105 condensed into 9 pages. This resource was made by me, so please do not reproduce.
I took CS2105 in AY2020/2021 Sem 2. This is supposed to be a simple module, so the bell curve was really steep. Most people scored full marks, or close to that, on the assingments and exams. The online midterm and final were open book and consisted of MCQ, MRQ and fill in the blank questions. Some of the questions were tricky, but most questions are similar to what has been seen in tutorials.
Overall, I felt that this module was dry and there was too much content. Every lecture simply dumped content onto us and I had to spend quite a lot of time looking through the concepts afterwards.
